MECHANISM OF ACTION
--------------------
Xeltarinib is a selective, irreversible inhibitor of mutant EGFR
(T790M/C797S double-mutant) with additional potent activity against
wild-type EGFR at clinical doses. Unlike first/second-generation TKIs,
it exploits a novel allosteric binding pocket (PharmaNova patent
WO2024/PN4782, filed 2024-02-15) adjacent to the ATP site.

Key differentiator: Covalent binding to Cys797 via a proprietary
acrylamide warhead with enhanced selectivity over VEGFR and PDGFR
(>200-fold selectivity ratio), which we hypothesise reduces the
cardiac toxicity profile seen with earlier-generation compounds.

SYNTHESIS PATHWAY (ABBREVIATED)

PRECLINICAL HEPATOTOXICITY DATA
----------------------------------
28-day rat study (doses 10, 30, 100 mg/kg/day):
  - 10 mg/kg: No LFT changes
  - 30 mg/kg: Transient ALT elevation (2x baseline, Day 14, resolved)
  - 100 mg/kg: ALT 8x baseline Day 21, hepatocellular necrosis on
    histology in 2/10 animals. NOAEL: 30 mg/kg.

13-week dog study (doses 5, 15, 50 mg/kg/day):
  - 50 mg/kg: Mild centrilobular hypertrophy, no necrosis. Reversible.
  - NOAEL: 15 mg/kg.

NOTE: Hepatotoxicity was noted in preclinical studies at high doses but
was NOT listed as an expected adverse reaction in the Investigator's
Brochure (IB v4.2) because clinical PK suggested human exposures at
75mg would remain below the animal NOAEL. The Bristol SUSAR (Grade 3
hepatotoxicity at 75mg clinical dose) represents an UNEXPECTED finding
that challenges this assumption. An urgent PK/PD re-analysis is underway.
