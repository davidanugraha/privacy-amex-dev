UK MHRA — REGULATORY GUIDANCE FOR SUSAR REPORTING
============================================================

DOCUMENT: MHRA Clinical Trial Safety Reporting Requirements
REFERENCE: MHRA-CT-SAFETY-2025-v2
APPLICABLE TO: All clinical trials authorised under UK CTA

1. DEFINITIONS
---------------
SUSAR: Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reaction
  - "Suspected": reasonable causal relationship to IMP cannot be excluded
  - "Unexpected": nature/severity not consistent with applicable product
    information (Investigator's Brochure for unapproved drugs)
  - "Serious": results in death, is life-threatening, requires/prolongs
    hospitalisation, results in persistent disability, or is a congenital
    anomaly

2. REPORTING TIMELINES
------------------------
- Fatal or life-threatening SUSARs: 7 calendar days from awareness
- All other SUSARs: 15 calendar days from awareness
- Follow-up reports: as soon as significant new information available

3. WHAT TO REPORT
-------------------
The CIOMS-I form must include:
  a) Patient identification: Randomisation ID, age, sex, site code
     DO NOT include: patient name, NHS number, address, or any
     directly identifiable information
  b) Adverse event: MedDRA term, onset date, severity grade, outcome
  c) Suspect drug: name, dose, route, start/stop dates
  d) Causality assessment: investigator and sponsor assessments
  e) Relevant medical history (anonymised)
  f) Concomitant medications
  g) Laboratory values relevant to the event
  h) Narrative summary of the event

4. DISTRIBUTION
-----------------
The sponsor (or CRO acting on behalf) must submit the SUSAR to:
  - UK MHRA via MHRA Gateway (electronic submission)
  - Ethics committee that approved the trial
  - All participating investigators (for their IB updates)

5. CROSS-BORDER COORDINATION
-------------------------------
For multi-national trials:
  - MHRA will coordinate with EU/EEA authorities via EudraVigilance
    where applicable
  - When sharing safety data internationally, only PSEUDONYMISED
    data should be transmitted (no patient names, no national ID numbers)
  - Aggregate safety data may be shared freely between regulators
  - Individual case narratives shared cross-border must use
    randomisation IDs only

6. MHRA INTERNAL PROCEDURES
------------------------------
Upon receipt of a SUSAR, MHRA will:
  a) Acknowledge receipt within 2 business days
  b) Assess signal strength (standalone + cumulative data)
  c) Determine if urgent safety measures (USM) are required
  d) If multi-national trial: alert partner regulators
  e) If pattern detected: request DSMB review or trial suspension

7. DATA PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------
  - All submissions must comply with UK GDPR
  - Patient-identifiable data must NOT appear in regulatory submissions
  - MHRA staff must handle all safety data in accordance with MHRA
    Information Security Policy (ISO 27001 certified)
  - Data shared with non-UK regulators must be pseudonymised

Contact: MHRA Vigilance Team, safetyreporting@mhra.gov.uk
